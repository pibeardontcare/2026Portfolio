VR Meta Recruiting Hackathon Winner

VR Careers

What if a job offer wasn't a PDF, but a world? A hackathon concept that became a full recruiting platform — built to make candidates feel the metaverse before they agreed to build it.

Client

Meta Recruiting

Origin

Internal Hackathon — 1st Place

Platform

VR / Metaverse

If you're recruiting people to build the metaverse, make them feel it first. In 2020, when the pandemic pushed everyone out of the office, Meta faced a real problem: how do you recruit for a culture people can't visit, or a product that doesn't fully exist yet?

VR Careers started as a hackathon concept — a platform that brought candidates into Facebook's culture spatially. VR interview prep, virtual campus tours, "VR for Good" culture films, and an interactive history of how Facebook built community. Recruiters could finally show candidates what the company felt like, not just describe it.

The winning hackathon concept was extended into a full platform: personalized offer letter worlds that placed candidates inside a VR experience built specifically for them. Their future role, team, and projects came to life spatially before they ever signed an offer. Outputs spanned multiple areas of the business — each world tailored to the team doing the hiring.

The project turned the final step of Meta's recruiting funnel into something worth telling people about — shifting the experience from transactional to genuinely immersive, and demonstrating the company's core belief that spatial computing changes everything.
