VR Synoptophore

Replacing a bulky clinical device with a portable VR diagnostic — precise enough for ophthalmologists, approachable enough for children.

VR Developer

The device this replaces

Traditional sensorimotor exams for binocular vision require a synoptophore — a large, expensive piece of clinical equipment that presents separate stimuli to each eye to assess ocular misalignment and binocular function. It requires a dedicated clinical setting, trained staff, and significant cost, creating access barriers worldwide.

This project replaces it entirely with a custom Meta Quest Pro application that mirrors the legacy workflow in VR: examiner-guided calibration, per-eye stimulus presentation, alignment measurement, and automated result logging — with comparative analysis against standard testing methods built in.

Clinical testing validated the approach at ±5.4 prism diopters accuracy — meeting clinical thresholds for meaningful assessment. The study was conducted in partnership with UC Davis Health Eye Center, supported by the J. William Kohl Summer Research Scholarship.

A pediatric ophthalmologist in the clinical study described the result plainly: "We enrolled a patient with strabismus. She wears 5 prism diopters to avoid double vision. Your software measured 5.4 prism diopters. Amazing." A formal Record of Invention has been submitted and patent proceedings are underway.

Workflow

Calibration → per-eye stimulus presentation → examiner-adjusted alignment measurement → automated response logging → summary with flagged discrepancies. Seated testing, minimal setup, examiner-controlled pace.
