P.E.C.K. — AR Pen Pals

Middle schoolers sharing culturally meaningful objects across continents as interactive AR models with their own voices attached.

Event

MIT Reality Hack

Audience

Middle School Students

Platform

Augmented Reality

Pen pals have always been about sharing your world with someone who's never seen it. P.E.C.K. (Pen Pal Exchange of Cultural Knowledge) is that idea rebuilt for spatial computing — letting kids share objects that matter to them not as photos, but as interactive 3D models that land on a student's desk on the other side of the world.

A student shares a family keepsake, a favorite meal, or a traditional cultural object. They record their voice explaining what it means. Their pen pal receives it as an AR model they can hold, rotate, and listen to — culture traveling as lived experience, not just image. Built on 8th Wall using Meshy's API for real-time 3D object generation from photos.

Built in 48 hours at the 2024 MIT Reality Hack with a team of four designers. The project pushes on one of the most meaningful use cases for AR: making the distant feel present, and the abstract feel real. For a generation growing up in a global but fragmented world, that matters.
