Salesforce × MrBeast

What started as a late-night prototype built in a Salesforce demo org became one of the most-watched brand activations of Super Bowl LIX.

Client

Salesforce

Collaborator

MrBeast

Launched

Super Bowl LIX, 2026

Role

Director, Creative Technology

I joined Salesforce in October after leaving Apple. In December, I heard we'd be partnering with MrBeast. When I learned they wanted to create a puzzle game for a Super Bowl commercial, I proposed reimagining it as a creative technology experience built on the Salesforce platform — interactive, dynamic, and scalable rather than a static one-off activation.

I spent several late nights working through Apex documentation and experimenting with custom actions until I had a working prototype running in a Salesforce demo org that showed how the concept could actually function. Adam Forrest gave me the chance to pitch that early prototype directly to MrBeast's team. They said yes.

What followed was one of the most intense stretches of collaboration I've been part of. Almost overnight, over 150 experts from across Salesforce jumped in. The timeline was nine weeks. Slack became a 24/7 war room across time zones — people volunteering expertise, solving problems in real time, pushing the limits of what an LLM-driven experience could do at Super Bowl scale. William Dressler led the technical vision and approached the timeline like it was solvable from day one. Mo kept project management across 50+ Slack channels aligned. Loan Shark Games designed a puzzle tough enough to live up to the moment.

What started as a late-night experiment became a shared mission. The kind of project that reminds you what's possible when you give people real ownership of a high bar.
